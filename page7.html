Sufi Shrines

The history of Sufiism in Pakistan goes back to over 1,000 years. Today, there are thousands of Sufi shrines and mausoleums spread in different parts of the country.

Some are famous among general public and some are unknown. For example, the graves of Sahaba Karam in Cholistan near Derawar Fort, Bahawalpur, or Wadi-e-Tarbela shrine.

Below is a list of some well-known shrines.

Hazrat Abdullah Shah Ghazi, Karachi
Hazrat Syed Abul Hassan Bin Usman Bin Ali Al-Hajweri, Lahore
Hazrat Makhdoom Baha-ud-din Zakariya, Hazrat Makhdoom Rukn-ud-din and Shah Shams-ud-Din Sabzwari, in Multan
Hazrat Fariduddin Ganjshakar, Pakpattan
Hazrat Syed Muhammad Usman (Lal Shahbaz Qalandar), Sehwan Sharif, Sindh 
Hazrat Jalaluddin Surkh-Posh Bukhari, and Hazrat Jahaniyan Jahangasht, Uch Sharif 
Hazrat Bibi Pak Daman, Lahore 
Hazrat Muhammad Qasim Sadiq, Mohra Sharif Murree 
Hazrat Meher Ali Shah, Golra Sharif, Islamabad 
Hazrat Abdul Wahab Faruqi (Sachal Sarmast) Khairpur Sindh 
hazrat Sultan Sakhi Sarwar Dera Ghazi Khan 
Hazrat Khwaja Ghulam Farid Mithankot Rajanpur  
Hazrat Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai Bhit Shah Matiari Sindh 
Hazrat Shah Abdul Latif Kazmi Islamabad 
Hazrat Pir Mangho Gadap Town Karachi Sindh 
Hazrat Sakhi Shah Chan Charagh Rawalpindi Punjab 

We offer region wise and city wise tours. For example, if you want to visit shrines in Rawalpindi Islamabad area, we will give you a list of all the shrines and you will decide where you want to go as per your time and your budget. 

In Islamabad, Loi dandi and Chillah Gah in Nilan Bhotu are the sites one must visit.
